Plot

Nine noble families fight for control over the lands of Westeros, while an ancient enemy returns after being dormant for millennia.

A popular misconception developed among viewers that the creators rushed the final season to start work on a Star Wars project. However, their plans for the final season were mapped out years before and the production actually took longer than previous seasons.

The final episodes required more time and effort — not less. The Star Wars project didn’t interfere, and the creators later left that deal for a major agreement with Netflix, which proves they weren't rushing to leave for Star Wars.
